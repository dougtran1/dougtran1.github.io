Branding

Dragon's Roast

Designer
Nov – Dec 2023
Academic Project
[ Hero image — Dragon's Roast logo on dark background ]

Overview

Dragon's Roast is a branding project for a fictional coffee shop built around a randomly assigned theme: fantasy. The goal was to design a full brand identity — logo, color palette, menu, and business card — within a one-month window using only in-class time.

The Challenge

Time was the biggest constraint. A week lost to Thanksgiving break compressed the already-tight schedule, forcing quick, confident decisions without sacrificing quality. The creative challenge was finding a visual language for "fantasy" that felt modern and warm rather than literal or clichéd.

Process

Moodboard

Research started with a moodboard pulling from medieval lore, dragon imagery, and specialty coffee aesthetics. The target vibe: warm, modern, with a nod to fantasy — not a costume shop, but a coffee shop that feels like it exists in a world with dragons.

Sketching

Early sketches leaned too literally into dragon wings and scales. After stepping back, the direction shifted toward something more abstract — a shape that could suggest a dragon without spelling it out.

[ Sketch explorations ]

Workshop & Feedback

Peer feedback was gathered around two questions:

  • Should the logo use an icon, wordmark, or a combination?
  • Should a color other than red and tan be added?

The majority favored a combined icon + wordmark approach. Feedback also pointed toward the complementary pairing of deep orange and dark navy as a strong direction.

"Icon design is clever, doesn't need to be a combination logo."
"Icon can stand on its own and can be recognizable on a cup or other merch."
"Color choice of the complementary oranges and dark blue fits your theme well."

Final Design

The final icon is a coffee bean shape merged with a flame — a subtle dragon reference without being on-the-nose. It reads as fire, coffee, and something mythical all at once. The wordmark pairs with the icon in a clean, spaced-out all-caps treatment.

Deliverables

The final brand system included:

  • Primary logo (icon + wordmark) and standalone icon
  • 5-color brand palette with CMYK, RGB, and HEX values
  • Multi-panel menu design
  • Front and back business card

Reflection

The peer review validated the direction without major revisions — a sign that early decisions landed well. In a future iteration, I'd explore textures like dragon scales or weathered armor to add depth, and spend more time generating multiple style directions (modern, gritty, rustic) early on to have more options to pull from.
